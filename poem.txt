"Whispers of the Unseen"

The river hums a nameless tune,
Beneath the silver eye of moon.
The baobab leans, ancient and wide,
To catch the secrets of the tide.

A broken drum beside the fire,
Still dreams of songs that never tire.
The wind, a dancer, wild and free,
Combs fingers through the mango tree.

Somewhere a child forgets to weep,
And sows a promise in the deep.
Somewhere a star, too faint to name,
Ignites a moth with timid flame.

So let the road turn where it will—
Through dust, through dawn, through hurt, through still.
The world has more than eyes can hold:
A story waiting to be told.
